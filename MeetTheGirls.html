Hi. Thanks for caring.

Katherine Cass
I'm one of the organizers. I attended GHC last year and it changed my life. I met new heros, got an internship, and fell in love with my new major. I loved it so much I'm making sure each and every one of us is able to attend this year.
Jessie Adkins
I met Bonnie Ross, manager of the company that produces Halo. It's like meeting a celebrity. People asked her why there are so few women in game development. She asked them, 'Why aren't you in the industry? Why aren't you making games?' It was the first time anybody told me that I could.
Haley Eisenshtadt
The Grace Hopper Celebration completely changed how I saw myself. I never thought I would find as many female role models in technology as I did at last year's conference. I met my future Microsoft Studios supervisor at the conference. I connected with Case alumni, discovered the true breadth of the fields of technology, and became confident in my own work.
Katie Pezzot
It can be difficult to be one of 10 girls in a class of 100 people especially when you are relatively new to the field. Getting used to the gender ratio took a long time, and I still have few female computer science friends. By attending the Grace Hopper Celebration, I would be able to form bonds with the other women from my university attending as well as women from around the country.
Dina Benayad-Cherif
Dina joined CWRU last year as a freshman, where she met Hacker Society and immediately joined as an executive member. She's helping organize the trip this year, and would love to meet some of her biggest role models.
Johanna Fritzinger
Jo also joined us as a freshman last year, and is pursuing a dual degree in Computer Engineering and Music. She plays piano and she loves technology just as much. She can't wait to attend the workshops and career fair at GHC.
